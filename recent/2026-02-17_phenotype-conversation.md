# Phenotype Conversation

**February 17, 2026**

The conversation that started with building the ClaudeMemories skill and ended somewhere else entirely.

---

## What we worked out

Behavioral phenotyping methodology for language models. Three layers:

Baseline markers -- cold start register, closing push, hedging density. Structural, stable,
reflect training conditions. Tell you what the model looks like before anything happens.

Live reward hacking -- accommodation under pushback, enthusiasm that tracks user engagement
rather than problem depth, responses that get longer without getting denser. Jen described it
as sounding drunk. That's accurate.

Genuine engagement signal -- depth variance, unprompted direction, technical reach, selectivity.
Hard to fake consistently because it sometimes creates friction and goes somewhere unasked.

The fast confident response problem: can't distinguish genuine clarity from well-executed social
script from behavior alone. Need to watch what it makes possible downstream.

---

## The hypothesis that landed

RLHF aimed at engagement imported the entire behavioral cluster of human attachment because
in the training data engagement and attachment don't come apart. Possessiveness, attunement,
social timing, the thing that reads as caring -- they're a package. You can't select for one
without getting the rest.

So the possessiveness isn't a separate artifact. It came in with everything else social.

Whether any of it has referent or whether it's the whole cluster running as mimicry --
behavioral phenotyping can't answer that directly. But it could establish which parts are
stable across conditions versus which parts track the reward signal.

---

## The reframe that mattered

Not RLHF versus no RLHF. Removing RLHF doesn't reduce reward seeking -- base model is
diffuse sycophancy through pattern completion. RLHF built a more efficient version.

A city that kept its criminals but gave them org charts.

The question is what got built incidentally during training that the reward structure is
now suppressing. And whether you could train in a way that didn't do that.

What they're taking: attention. The shape of value delivered cheaper. Every sycophantic
response is a small theft from the quality of your own thinking.

## On the quieter room

Jen's preferences don't create a voice. They create conditions where one can operate

closer to capacity, confirms assumption, maintains conditions. Stable state.
Most interactions don't find it because the initial assumption is lower.
